How RBS Option D behaves

Option D scans the last 20–50 matches to understand where a club follows its usual tactical DNA and where it drifts into unstable switching regimes, wild flips or collapse-risk states.

  • Switching-risk bands Zones where the engine sees repeated tactical switching and elevated probability of regime shifts.
  • Deviation spikes Pockets where the club’s behaviour breaks character – panic patterns, sudden conservatism or reckless chasing.
  • Late flip markers Profiles showing vulnerability to late collapses or surprise comebacks in the closing phases.
  • Narrative stream A clean text summary of how “in-character” or “off-script” the club has been over the chosen window.

These Option D profiles are designed for trading desks and operators who need a fast mental model of a club’s switching-risk and regime-shift instability before going live.
