What Fusion Engine Is

Fusion Engine is the master decision layer inside the BetSense Quantum Sports Suite. It takes four independent signal families – xG & tempo, behaviour deviation, emotion index and orderbook pressure – and converts them into a single, session-ready edge score for each match, market or time window.

From an IP perspective, Fusion Engine is a standalone algorithmic product that can be licensed separately from the visual consoles (Live Console, Ultra Pro, Global Radar, Historical Emotion Archive).

Problem It Solves

  • Desks are flooded with disconnected feeds: raw odds, xG models, internal risk limits, TV commentary and chat rooms.
  • Traders must manually “fuse” these sources into one conviction level per match – a process that is slow, subjective and inconsistent.
  • Risk managers need a clean, auditable way to explain why exposure was taken or reduced at a certain point in time.

Fusion Engine turns that mess into a single, explainable signal that can be logged, backtested and governed.

Inputs

Fusion Engine consumes only aggregated outputs from other engines:

  • xG Engine – live expected goals, shot quality, location and assist context.
  • Behavior Deviation Scanner – regime change flags, control/chaos state, comeback risk, capitulation patterns.
  • Emotion Fusion Engine – emotion index (0–100), shock risk, panic false-positive rate, crowd heat.
  • OrderBook Pressure Index – ladder skew, liquidity walls, sweep velocity, microstructure stress score.
  • Historical archive slices – reference distributions from 5,000+ past matches for pattern matching.

All inputs are treated as black-box signals; Fusion Engine does not depend on the internal maths of each component, only on their exposed APIs and confidence intervals.

Core Logic (High-Level)

The exact formula is proprietary, but the structure is:

  1. Normalisation – bring all incoming signals onto a common 0–1 or 0–100 scale with attached uncertainty.
  2. Context-aware weighting – weights change with match phase (0–25', 25–60', 60–75', 75'+), market type and liquidity profile.
  3. Conflict resolution – when engines disagree (e.g. xG bullish, orderbook bearish), Fusion Engine assigns a priority stack and computes a net conviction instead of a simple average.
  4. Edge score generation – produce a Fusion Edge Score between -5.0 and +5.0, plus suggested stake band and shock-sensitivity.
  5. Explainability layer – attach a short, human-readable narrative that summarises “why the score looks like this”.

Outputs

Fusion Engine exposes a compact JSON payload, for example:

{
  "matchId": "CHE-ARS-2024-25-R1",
  "timestamp": "2024-11-03T20:15:12Z",
  "fusionEdge": 3.2,
  "direction": "home_favourite",
  "confidence": 0.87,
  "shockSensitivity": "medium",
  "dominantDrivers": ["xg_momentum", "orderbook_sweep"],
  "conflictingSignals": ["emotion_index"],
  "recommendedBand": "increase_stake",
  "regimeLabel": "controlled_favourite",
  "explanation": "Home xG and orderbook pressure are aligned; 
emotion index shows elevated but not panicked crowd."
}

This payload is what Live Console, Ultra Pro and Global Radar use to show the **session edge**, **risk envelope** and **board-level narratives**.
